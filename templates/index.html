What do these parameters mean?
Engine RPM

Engine revolutions per minute. Higher RPM values typically increase fuel consumption. The economic range is usually 1500-2500 RPM.

Speed

Vehicle speed in kilometers per hour. Most vehicles achieve optimal fuel efficiency between 50-80 km/h.

Throttle Position

Measures how far the accelerator pedal is pressed, shown as a percentage. Gentle throttle application (below 50%) is usually more fuel-efficient.

Acceleration

Rate of change in speed, measured in meters per second squared. Positive values indicate acceleration, negative values indicate deceleration. Smooth, gradual acceleration (0.5-1.5 m/s²) is typically more economical.

Engine Load

Indicates how hard the engine is working relative to its maximum capacity, shown as a percentage. Lower engine loads generally result in better fuel economy.
